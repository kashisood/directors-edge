📖 The story

Email is deceptively deep: billions of mailboxes, huge messages, full-text search, spam, and the dark art of deliverability — getting your mail accepted by other servers.

So bodies go to object storage with a per-user search index, sending runs through a queue that manages retries and sender reputation, and spam filtering happens asynchronously off the critical path.

How to think about it — step by step

Respect how deep email is

Storage, search, spam, and deliverability all matter. Why: naming the parts shows you won't underestimate it.

Store bodies cheaply, index for search

Blobs in object storage; a per-user search index. Why: mailboxes are huge and people search them constantly.

Send through a queue

Manage retries, backoff, and sender reputation. Why: sending too fast or to bad addresses wrecks deliverability.

Filter spam off the path

Classify inbound and outbound asynchronously. Why: it shouldn't slow delivery.

Know the real hard part

Deliverability — SPF/DKIM/DMARC, warm IPs, throttling. Why: getting other servers to accept your mail is the actual challenge.

1 · Requirements

Functional: send and receive email; per-user mailboxes; search; spam handling. Non-functional: massive storage, reliable delivery, good deliverability.

2 · Capacity

Billions of mailboxes; messages can be large with attachments — bodies live in object storage, with a search index per user.

3 · Architecture

4 · The hard parts

  • Storage + search: blob bodies + a per-user inverted index; shard by user.
  • Deliverability: warm IPs, SPF/DKIM/DMARC, throttle per recipient domain, monitor bounces/complaints — reputation is everything.
  • Spam: async classification both inbound and outbound.

5 · Trade-offs

  • Index everything (fast search, storage/compute cost) vs lazy indexing.
  • Send throughput vs protecting sender reputation (must throttle).

Key sub-systems

  • Ingestion (SMTP/API) — accepts mail.
  • Object storage — message bodies.
  • Search index — per-user, sharded.
  • Send queue — retries, reputation.
  • Spam filter — async, in/outbound.

Bottlenecks & what to optimize next

  • Deliverability is the real bottleneck — warm IPs, throttle per domain, monitor bounces.
  • Search over huge mailboxes — shard the index per user.

Common follow-up questions

Why is deliverability hard?

Other mail servers judge your sender reputation; sending too fast or to bad addresses gets you throttled or blocked. You warm IPs and throttle per recipient domain.

Where do message bodies live?

In object storage, with a per-user inverted index for search; the DB holds metadata.

How do you fight spam?

Async classifiers on inbound and outbound, plus SPF/DKIM/DMARC authentication and bounce/complaint monitoring.
