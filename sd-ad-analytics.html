📖 The story

When clicks bill money, counting them is anything but trivial: billions of events, duplicates, and stragglers that arrive late and out of order — yet the totals must be both fast and ultimately exact.

The standard answer blends two paths: a real-time stream for instant approximate counts, and a batch path that reconciles for accuracy — the lambda architecture — serving from an OLAP store.

How to think about it — step by step

Respect that it bills money

Counts must be both fast and ultimately exact. Why: approximate-only won't do for billing.

Ingest as a stream

Events flow into Kafka. Why: a durable log absorbs billions of events and lets you reprocess.

Use two paths (lambda)

A stream processor gives real-time approximate counts; a batch job reconciles for accuracy. Why: you get speed now and correctness later.

Handle messy reality

Dedupe by event ID; use watermarks for late, out-of-order events. Why: duplicates and stragglers would corrupt the count.

Serve from OLAP

Pre-aggregate rollups in a columnar store. Why: dashboards need fast slice-and-dice queries.

1 · Requirements

Functional: ingest ad/click events; aggregate counts by dimension/time; serve dashboards and billing. Non-functional: billions/day, near-real-time, and accurate (it bills money).

3 · The hard parts

  • Accurate counting: dedupe (event IDs) and handle late/out-of-order events with watermarks & windows; exactly-once for billing.
  • Real-time vs accurate: the stream gives speed (approximate); the batch path gives the authoritative number.
  • Cardinality: pre-aggregate rollups by common dimensions to keep queries fast.

4 · Trade-offs

  • Real-time (fast, approximate) vs batch (accurate, delayed) — lambda blends both.
  • Exactly-once (correct, costly) vs at-least-once + dedup.

Key sub-systems

  • Event ingest — Kafka.
  • Stream processor — Flink, real-time aggregates.
  • Batch processor — Spark, reconciliation.
  • OLAP store — Druid/ClickHouse.
  • Dedup/idempotency — for billing accuracy.

Bottlenecks & what to optimize next

  • Accuracy under duplicates/late events is the challenge — dedup + watermarks; batch reconciles.
  • Cardinality of dimensions — pre-aggregate rollups to keep queries fast.

Common follow-up questions

How do you count accurately for billing?

Dedup by event ID and reconcile with a batch path; real-time gives speed, batch gives the authoritative number (lambda architecture).

How do you handle late events?

Watermarks and windowing in the stream processor so stragglers land in the correct time bucket.

Why an OLAP store?

Dashboards need fast slice-and-dice over huge data; columnar OLAP engines pre-aggregate rollups for that.

🧠 Remember it

Ad analytics = stream + batch (lambda), exactly-once for billing.

  • Kafka → Flink (real-time) + Spark (reconcile) → OLAP.
  • Dedupe + watermarks handle duplicates & late events.
  • Pre-aggregate rollups for fast queries.
