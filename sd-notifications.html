📖 The story

A notification system sits between ‘something happened’ and a buzz on a phone. The work — preferences, rate limits, templates, delivery — is best done off the hot path, asynchronously.

So events land on a queue; stateless workers apply preferences and rate limits, render templates, and hand off to channel adapters (push/email/SMS). Dedup and retries make delivery reliable despite flaky providers.

How to think about it — step by step

Get the work off the hot path

Sending isn't instant or critical to the second, so don't block on it. Why: a queue lets you absorb spikes and retry.

Use a queue + workers

Events go on a queue; workers apply preferences, render templates, and send. Why: decouples 'something happened' from 'deliver it'.

Fan out to channels

Adapters talk to push (APNs/FCM), email, and SMS providers. Why: each channel has its own quirks; isolate them.

Make delivery reliable

At-least-once delivery + a dedupe key so retries don't spam users; failures go to a dead-letter queue. Why: external providers fail, so plan for it.

Respect the human

Honor opt-outs, quiet hours, and rate caps. Why: notification fatigue is a real product failure.

1 · Requirements

Functional: send push, email, SMS, and in-app notifications; respect user preferences & quiet hours. Non-functional: high volume, reliable delivery, seconds of latency acceptable.

2 · Capacity

Millions/min in bursts — a queue absorbs spikes and decouples producers from delivery.

4 · The hard parts

  • Reliability: at-least-once delivery + idempotent dedup keys so users aren’t spammed on retries.
  • Provider failures: retries with backoff, a dead-letter queue, and per-provider circuit breakers.
  • Preferences & throttling: respect opt-outs, quiet hours, and per-user rate caps (notification fatigue).

5 · Trade-offs

  • At-least-once + dedup (simple) vs exactly-once (complex).
  • Immediate send vs batching/digest (less noise, more delay).

Key sub-systems

  • Ingestion API — accepts events to notify on.
  • Queue — Kafka, absorbs spikes.
  • Workers — preferences, rate limits, templating.
  • Channel adapters — APNs/FCM, email, SMS.
  • Dedupe + retry — idempotency keys, DLQ.

Bottlenecks & what to optimize next

  • Provider rate limits/outages are the bottleneck — queue, backoff, and circuit-break per provider.
  • Fan-out for broadcast notifications — batch and shard workers.

Common follow-up questions

How do you avoid spamming users on retries?

At-least-once delivery plus an idempotency/dedupe key so a retried send is recognized and dropped.

How do you handle a provider outage?

Retry with backoff into a dead-letter queue, circuit-break that provider, and optionally fail over to an alternate.

How do you respect user preferences?

Workers check per-user channel preferences, quiet hours, and rate caps before sending.

🧠 Remember it

Notifications = queue → workers → channel adapters, reliable + deduped.

  • At-least-once + idempotent dedup; retries + DLQ.
