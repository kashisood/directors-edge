📖 The story

Food delivery juggles three impatient parties — the diner, the restaurant, and the courier — in real time. It borrows ride-hailing’s geospatial matching and adds a marketplace and an order lifecycle.

So an order service drives a state machine, a courier-matching service queries a geo index for nearby drivers, and ETAs blend kitchen prep time with travel time.

How to think about it — step by step

Count the parties

Diner, restaurant, courier — all in real time. Why: coordination is the theme.

Drive it with an order state machine

placed → accepted → preparing → picked-up → delivered, via events. Why: a clear lifecycle keeps three parties in sync.

Reuse geo matching

Courier assignment is the ride-hailing problem. Why: connect to what you know instead of inventing.

Get ETAs right

Combine kitchen prep time with travel time. Why: both are moving estimates that affect the promise to the diner.

Name the trade-off

Assign one order per courier (fast) vs batch nearby orders (efficient, slower). Why: a real business lever.

3 · The hard parts

  • Three-sided coordination: an event-driven order state machine keeps diner, restaurant, and courier in sync.
  • Courier matching: geospatial index (geohash/QuadTree) finds nearby couriers, ranked by ETA; lock on offer to avoid double-assignment.
  • ETA: combine kitchen prep time with travel time; both are estimates that update.
  • Batching: assign one courier multiple nearby orders to improve efficiency.

4 · Trade-offs

  • Assignment for speed (one order per courier) vs efficiency (batched deliveries, slower).
  • Availability consistency vs latency.

Key sub-systems

  • Order service — lifecycle state machine.
  • Restaurant service — menu + availability.
  • Courier matching — geospatial, like Uber.
  • ETA service — prep + travel time.
  • Payments — idempotent.

Bottlenecks & what to optimize next

  • Coordinating three parties in real time — an event-driven state machine keeps them in sync.
  • Courier efficiency — batching nearby orders trades speed for utilization.

Common follow-up questions

How is this different from ride-hailing?

Same geospatial matching for couriers, plus a marketplace (restaurants/menus) and a longer order lifecycle (prep + pickup + delivery).

How do you estimate delivery time?

Combine kitchen prep time (per restaurant, time-of-day) with courier travel time; both are live estimates.

How do you improve courier efficiency?

Batch nearby orders to one courier — better utilization at the cost of slightly slower individual deliveries.
