📖 The story

Money is the one domain where ‘mostly correct’ is a disaster. The entire design exists to guarantee that every cent is accounted for, twice, and that no failure or retry ever creates or destroys money.

So the source of truth is a double-entry ledger — every movement is a balanced pair of entries you can audit forever. Idempotency keys make retries safe, sagas handle the bank that you can’t transactionally control, and a reconciliation job assumes drift will happen and catches it.

How to think about it — step by step

Adopt the mindset: correctness first

Never lose or double-count money. Why: this domain trades speed for certainty, unlike most.

Make the ledger the truth

A double-entry, append-only ledger; balances are derived, never edited. Why: every cent is auditable and nothing is silently overwritten.

Make retries safe

An idempotency key returns the first result instead of charging again. Why: networks retry, and a double charge is unacceptable.

Coordinate the bank with a saga

You can't transactionally control an external bank, so do the step and compensate (reverse) on failure. Why: distributed steps need a recovery story.

Assume drift, then catch it

A daily reconciliation compares your ledger to the bank. Why: trust nothing; verify.

1 · Requirements

Functional: charge a customer, transfer funds, refund; show balance & history. Non-functional: correctness above all (never lose or double-count money), full auditability, strong consistency for balances, and resilience to retries and partial failures. Throughput is secondary to getting every cent right.

2 · Capacity

Even at thousands of transactions/sec the volume is modest; the difficulty is exactly-once money movement across services and external banks that can time out, retry, or fail mid-flow.

3 · API

  • POST /v1/payments with a client Idempotency-Key → charge; replaying the same key returns the original result, never a second charge.
  • POST /v1/refunds, GET /v1/accounts/{id}/balance, GET /v1/accounts/{id}/transactions.

4 · Data model

  • Double-entry ledger (source of truth): every movement is two entries (debit one account, credit another) that must sum to zero — ledger_entry(txnId, accountId, direction, amount, currency, ts).
  • Idempotency keys: idempotency(key, requestHash, resultRef, status).
  • Balances are derived/materialized from the ledger, not edited in place.

6 · The hard parts

  • Exactly-once: the idempotency key makes a retried request return the first result instead of charging twice.
  • Distributed transaction: you can’t two-phase-commit an external bank, so use a saga — do the step, and on failure run a compensating action (reverse the ledger entry).
  • Auditability: the append-only double-entry ledger means every balance is provable and nothing is ever silently overwritten.
  • Reconciliation: assume drift happens; a daily job catches and flags mismatches against the bank.

7 · Scaling & failure

  • Partition the ledger by account; keep a single writer per account to preserve consistency.
  • Replicate synchronously across AZs — for money you accept higher latency for durability.
  • On crash mid-saga, a recovery process resumes or compensates from the saga log; idempotency makes replays safe.

8 · Trade-offs

  • Correctness & auditability over latency/throughput — the rare domain where you pick consistency every time.
  • Saga (eventual consistency across services) over impossible cross-system 2PC.
  • Async fraud (don’t block the charge) with the ability to reverse later.

Key sub-systems

  • Payment API — idempotency-key entry point.
  • Ledger — double-entry, append-only, ACID.
  • Saga orchestrator — external bank steps + compensation.
  • Fraud service — async scoring.
  • Reconciliation — daily vs bank settlement.

Bottlenecks & what to optimize next

  • Correctness is the constraint, not throughput — single-writer per account, synchronous replication.
  • External bank failures — sagas with compensating reversals; reconcile to catch drift.

Common follow-up questions

How do you guarantee no double-charge on retry?

An idempotency key — a replayed request returns the original result instead of charging again.

Why a double-entry ledger?

Every movement is a balanced pair of append-only entries, so balances are derivable, auditable, and never silently overwritten.

How do you handle a step failing at the bank?

A saga: do each step and, on failure, run a compensating action (reverse the ledger entry); reconcile daily against the bank.
